Research Interests

My research interests lie broadly in algorithms and complexity. The following is a small subset of the interesting problems I've spent time looking at in the past:

Graph Theory:

In Faster Detours in Undirected Graphs [/], the authors state, "it remains open whether k-Longest Detour is in P even for the special case of k=1!" My most recent work has explored this problem by considering reductions to bridge-finding.

Algorithmic Number Theory:

In The Role of Smooth Numbers in Number Theoretic Algorithms [/], Carl Pomerance discusses how smooth numbers are central to many algorithms in number theory. In particular, such numbers are generally easier to factor, which suggests a kind of hierarchy of integers with respect to factoring difficulty: numbers with certain structural properties are easier to factor than others. Intriguingly, this hierarchy appears robust under classical assumptions but collapses in the quantum setting, as demonstrated by Shor’s algorithm [/]. I’m interested in exploring other “special” classes of numbers to better understand this hierarchy.

Fuzzy Extactors:

In Fuzzy Extractors: How to Generate Strong Keys from Biometrics and Other Noisy Data [/], the authors present a new cryptographic primitive called fuzzy extractors. In my undergraduate thesis, I constructed fuzzy classifiers which are wrappers that use lattice-based fuzzy extractors for authentication systems. More recently, I've been looking into providing theoretical bounds for the loss of entropy when going from distance-based classifiers to lattice-based classifiers.
